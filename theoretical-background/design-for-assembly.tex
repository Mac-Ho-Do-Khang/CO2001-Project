\section{Design for Assembly}

Design for Assembly (DFA) is an engineering approach that focuses on designing products so that they can be assembled easily, quickly, and with fewer errors. The goal is to simplify the assembly process by reducing the number of parts, minimizing the need for special tools, and making components easy to handle and align. By considering assembly during the design stage, engineers can lower production costs, shorten assembly time, and improve product reliability.

Design for Assembly is closely related to its “brother”, Design for Manufacturing (DFM). While DFA focuses on simplifying the assembly of parts, DFM concentrates on making each part easier and more cost-effective to manufacture.
