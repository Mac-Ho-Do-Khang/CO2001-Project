\section*{\Huge Abstract}

Rapid prototyping technologies have significantly transformed modern engineering design and product development processes. Among these technologies, Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) 3D printing enables fast fabrication of customized mechanical components with relatively low cost and short production time.

At the same time, embedded systems based on microcontrollers such as the ESP32 have become widely used in prototyping electronic devices due to their high performance, integrated communication capabilities, and ease of programming.

Despite the availability of powerful development boards, many embedded systems are typically implemented in temporary setups without a dedicated enclosure. This limits their usability as complete functional devices and makes system integration less efficient.

This project aims to integrate embedded system development with mechanical design and additive manufacturing techniques. Specifically, the project proposes the design and development of a device based on the TTGO T-Display ESP32 board combined with a custom-designed 3D-printed housing. Through this integration, the project demonstrates how electronic hardware, embedded programming, and digital fabrication can be combined to produce a compact and functional device.
