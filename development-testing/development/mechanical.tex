\subsection{Mechanical Design}

With the board's geometry fully characterised, the mechanical design phase produces the custom 3D-printed housing. The workflow within this phase proceeds through four successive steps.

\subsubsection{CAD Modelling}

The enclosure is modelled in Autodesk Fusion 360. The internal cavity is dimensioned to match the board footprint, while external features, including 

\begin{itemize}
    \item Side-wall apertures for the USB-C port and buttons
    \item Front-face opening for the display
\end{itemize}

are positioned to align with the results of the system analysis phase.

The entire enclosure is consist of three components:

\begin{itemize}
    \item A rectangular backshell that houses the board and the battery. This is the main structural component that provides support and protection for the whole product.
    \item A front cover that snaps onto the backshell, securing the board in place and providing an opening for the display.
    \item A small button providing access to the reset board's reset pin.
\end{itemize}

\subsubsection{Slicing and Print-Parameter Configuration}

The finalised CAD model is exported and imported into Ultimaker Cura for slicing. Slicing is the process of converting the 3D model into a series of 2D layers, which are then translated into G-code instructions that the 3D printer can execute.

Print parameters including temperature, layer height, infill density and print speed are configured to balance structural integrity, dimensional accuracy, and print time.

Support is a feature where extra material is printed to hold up overhanging parts during the printing process. Each part is oriented in a way that minimises the need for support, as support material can increase print time and post-processing effort. For example, the backshell is printed with the open side facing upwards to avoid the need for support under the internal cavity. This is another application of DFM principles, as it optimises the design for the manufacturing process.

\subsubsection{FDM Printing}

The sliced G-code files are transferred to a Creality Ender 3 V2 printer. As mentioned above, PLA filament is selected for its ease of printing, good dimensional stability, and sufficient mechanical strength for a non-load-bearing enclosure application. After printing, the parts undergo basic post-processing before proceeding to the integration phases.
